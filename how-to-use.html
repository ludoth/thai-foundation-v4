🌺 How to use

Thai Foundation v3 · Complete guide

🧠
The idea

This app helps you learn Thai vocabulary from the "Thai Foundation Course" book. It shows you a word or phrase — you try to recall the translation — then reveal the answer and tell the app how you did.

The app uses Spaced Repetition (SRS): it tracks every card and brings hard cards back sooner, easy cards back later. Over time you spend time only on what you actually need to practice.

🔄
Direction

Eng → Thai — See English, recall Thai. Best for beginners.

Thai → Eng — See Thai romanisation + script, recall English. Harder and more realistic.

💡 Start with Eng → Thai. Switch to Thai → Eng once you're comfortable with a category.
⚙️
Session settings

Session size — how many cards per session:

Quick · 10 — a fast warm-up, great for daily habit.
Normal · 20 — the default, balanced session.
Full — all cards currently due, no limit.

New cards per session — the slider controls how many brand-new (never-seen) cards are introduced each session. Set it low (5) while learning, higher (15–20) to go faster.

💡 New cards mixed into review sessions are the highest-effort cards. If sessions feel overwhelming, lower the new-cards slider.
📚
Categories

20+ categories from the book — Numbers, Verbs, Greetings, Grammar, Family, Animals, Food & Drinks, and more. Select one or several, then press Start.

Each category button shows a green mastered count and red remaining count on the right.

The 🎲 Random button adds 20 cards picked at random from all categories — great for a mixed challenge once you've studied several categories.

💡 Start with 2–3 categories. Add more as you master them.

Back — the solution

A New badge appears on cards you've never seen before.

The back shows the Thai romanisation (large) and Thai script below it. Hover over any script character to see its sound.

Tap the card or press Solution to reveal the answer. Then honestly rate yourself:

✓ Got it — you knew it. Card advances in SRS.
✗ Again — you didn't. Card comes back this session (max 3 times).
Skip — defer without penalty. Card returns once later in the session.

💡 Be honest. Only press Got it if you remembered before peeking — not after seeing the answer.

Progress bar

Seen (dark) — cards you've answered at least once correctly. This grows quickly.

Mastered (amber) — cards that have reached the highest SRS box. These are deeply learned.

🟠
Learning dots (SRS boxes)

Six dots below the card show which SRS box that card is in:

Box 1 — new, seen every session
Box 3 — good, shown every 4 sessions
Box 6 — mastered, shown every 16 sessions

✓ Got it moves the card one box forward. ✗ Again resets it to box 1 (but only re-queues it 3 times per session max).

↩️
Undo

The ↩ Undo button (top-right of the study screen) reverses your last answer — restoring the card's SRS state and session stats exactly as they were before.

Only one level of undo is available. It clears after each new card.

💡 Use undo when you accidentally pressed Got it on a word you didn't actually know, or pressed Again when you knew it fine.
💤
Suspend a card

The 💤 button (top-left of the study screen) suspends the current card — removing it from all future sessions until you clear it.

Use this for cards that are too advanced, irrelevant, or that you want to revisit much later.

On the picker screen you'll see how many cards are suspended, with a clear all link to restore them.

🔊
Audio

The 🔊 button reads the word currently visible on the card, in the correct language. It reads English when the English face is showing and Thai when the solution is showing.

Audio requires a Thai voice installed on your device. If it doesn't work:

iPhone / Mac: Settings → Accessibility → Spoken Content → Voices → Thai → download a voice.
Android: Settings → General Management → Language → Text-to-Speech → install Thai.

🇹🇭
Thai script tooltips

On the back of every card (and on the front in Thai → Eng mode), hover over any Thai script character to see its approximate phonetic sound.

These are single-character approximations — actual pronunciation depends on context and tone. Use them as a guide, not a rule.

→ For a full pronunciation guide, see Pronunciation Guide.

🔥
Streak

The 🔥 5 pill tracks your consecutive daily study days. Even a 10-card Quick session counts.

💡 Consistency beats intensity. 10 minutes every day beats 2 hours once a week.
📱
Install on your phone

iPhone: Open in Safari → tap Share → Add to Home Screen.

Android: Open in Chrome → tap menu (⋮) → Add to Home Screen.

All progress is saved locally on your device.
