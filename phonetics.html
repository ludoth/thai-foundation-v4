🗣️ Pronunciation Guide

How to read the phonetic Thai in this app

📖
What system does this app use?

The app uses the Thai Foundation Course book's own phonetic system — a simplified romanisation designed for English speakers. It is not the official RTGS system used in dictionaries.

The goal is practical pronunciation, not linguistic precision. Once you understand the patterns, you can read any word in the app correctly.

Hover over any Thai script letter on a card to see its approximate sound.

🎵
The 5 tones — the most important thing

Thai is a tonal language. The same syllable said in 5 different tones means 5 completely different things. Tones are marked with diacritic symbols above vowels.

Mid a no mark, flat
Low à starts low, stays low
Falling â starts high, falls
High á starts high, stays
Rising ǎ dips then rises

Example: maa = come (mid) · mâa = horse (falling) · màa = dog (low)

💡 Don't stress about tones at first. Focus on getting the vowel and consonant sounds right. Tones come naturally with listening practice. The 🔊 button helps you hear the correct tone.

Consonant sounds — the tricky ones

Most consonants sound as expected. These are the ones that confuse English speakers:

WrittenSounds likeNote
ph"p" as in potNOT "f" — it's an aspirated p
th"t" as in topNOT "th" in the — it's an aspirated t
kh"k" as in kingAspirated k, like a soft "kh"
ng"ng" as in singCan appear at the START of a word — e.g. ngoo (snake)
bpbetween "b" and "p"Unaspirated — softer than English p
dtbetween "d" and "t"Unaspirated — softer than English t
j"j" as in jumpSame as English
ch"ch" as in chairSame as English
rrolled "r"Often sounds like "l" in casual speech
final -kunaspirated stopDon't release the k — just stop the air
final -tunaspirated stopSame — stop the air, no release
final -punaspirated stopSame — lips close but no burst
💡 The key insight: Thai has aspirated (with a puff of air) and unaspirated consonants. ph, th, kh = aspirated (air puff). Plain p, t, k = no air puff.

Reading Thai script

Thai script reads left to right, but vowels can appear above, below, before, or after their consonant. There are no spaces between words.

In this app, hover over any Thai character on the back of a card to see its approximate sound. This is a learning aid — individual character sounds don't always match pronunciation in context due to tonal rules and vowel combinations.

💡 The best way to learn Thai script is to study it systematically alongside this app. The romanisation in this app will get you speaking — the script will take longer but is deeply rewarding.
